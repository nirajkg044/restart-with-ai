Top Large Language Models

Large Language Models (LLMs) are transformer-based neural networks trained on massive text corpora to approximate the probability distribution of language sequences. Given tokens x₁…xₙ the model predicts the next token using P(xₜ | x<t). Self-attention layers compute contextual relationships using Attention(Q,K,V)=softmax(QKᵀ/√d)V. With billions or trillions of parameters these models learn representations that enable reasoning, coding, summarization, and autonomous AI agent behavior. This page provides a developer-focused overview of major LLM systems currently used in AI engineering.
